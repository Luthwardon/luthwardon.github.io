Hosting Types

Wiki > Player guide > Hosting types

A few different types of hosts that plot staff may do.

Free Roam

"Do whatever you want".

  • No DM required.
  • Stat systems are not implemented.
  • Dungeons and quests are disabled.
  • Encounters and interactions are available.

Story Mode

Most hosts will be Story Mode.

  • Hosted by a DM.
  • Dungeons, quests and encounters.
  • Stat system and combat system.

Quests, Dungeons, & Encounters

Wiki > Player guide > Content

There are things to do scattered throughout Luthwardron. Some may be locked depending on your actions, affiliations, race, etc.

Quests

Some NPCs will give out quests for players. Most quests will give you a book with a list of objectives to complete. How you complete these objectives is up to you.

Big quests will have to be overseen by a DM, due to interactions and events during those quests. For these quests a party is needed.

Dungeons

Scattered throughout the land will be dungeons that you can progress through. At the end there is loot to be had that your character can then use, as well as “level ups” to increase your stats. Some forts and other structures can act as dungeons if you decide to attack them. Quests may have dungeons attached to them.

A DM is required to oversee encounters in dungeons

Some dungeons and boss rooms will not fit on the plot. In that case there will be another plot where the dungeon / boss room will be built.

Encounters

There are lots of NPC armor stands throughout the land of Luthwardron. You may interact with them as you want. However, there are a few exceptions.

Shopkeepers sell items and tools. To purchase stuff from them you must contact a DM if they are online.

Guards & Soldiers may be killed. You must get a DM to oversee this. If not planned out, you may get swarmed by guards. The DM will act out the actions of the NPCs you are fighting.

Monsters may be encountered in the wilderness. There will be some signs in front of creatures in caves and places. If the sign is in hallway you must fight those creatures in order to pass. There will be a build/armor stand there as well attempting to depict the monsters. If the monsters require a DM, it will be stated on the sign.

Combat

Wiki > Player guide > Combat

Combat is turn-based, similar to D&D.

  1. Roll for initiative or decide the order people go.
  2. Don't spam. After you do your emote wait your turn for the next person.
  3. Dice can be used with modifiers if people can't agree to how rp goes.
  4. Don't take it too seriously, this is for fun! If you die, you can respawn.
