Welcome, to Luthwardron, the center of the universe.

Wayward souls, and Wanderlost travelers, oft find themselves wandering the roads of this great Realm.

All Roads Lead to Luthwardron.

The Magicks Flourish among its lands, Interwoven and Interlinked with its diverse Peoples.

In days long past, Ancient Evils soaked the Realm in an unholy blood.

The Vessel of God rose to defeat them.

Those days have long since passed.

Stories of fantastical wars and godly vessels of Wonder are all silenced by the Empire.

Perhaps, they were never real to begin with.

Find your Home, within the Empire of Man! Perhaps, you fancy an Elven Life? Or the call of the Dwarven Mines?

Seek out the secrets of this land, rise as a Hero of Good, or a Champion of the Demon King.

Fate is, as always, in your hands.

Be guided, not by fear, but by wonder.

Pax Luthwardia,

And may The Divinity treat you well.

Overview

Home page

The Universe, oft referred to by the scholars of old as The Divinity.

The Divinity encompasses all of this; All sects of this plane. No corner untouched.

The Divinity is above all intellect. Its reason is beyond all mind. That is all it can ever be.

The Realms of this plane are vast. Vivid spectrums of unintelligible distance- whose comprehension spells madness, if not death, for any mortal mind.

At the center of this Realm, lies a Nexus. A transcendental hub of;

Power;

Life;

Good;

Evil;

And all other things that can be understood. This center is known as Luthwardron.

Luthwardron is the gathering point for many things. All roads that travel across The Divinity tend to begin, and end, in Luthwardron. Missing people, oft find themselves wandering its streets. Lost souls gather among its graves. Those without purpose tend to find it within the now-lively Kingdoms- But it was not always this way. Eons ago, time immemorial, Luthwardron was, as it is now, the Nexus for all things- and by virtue of this- it was a Realm of War.

Forces of such power, that merely speaking their long-forgotten names is of grave danger to the mortal man- did fierce battle on the grounds of Luthwardron.

One force rose above the rest.

The Dark Lord.

The Harbinger of Doom.

The Limit of Evil.

Known by name, to only his most loyal servants, as the High King Rafaent. [placeholder evil name]. (subject for change).

The Primordial Evil was a creature born of uncertainty- from the most vile of mortal emotions- the darkest parts of every mind- and the one, true fear of all living things. The Void.

His reign was painted with horror. His cunning and awe-inspiring power brought the realm of Luthwardron to its knees- but Crimson was not satisfied. His hunger was unquenched, and he reached outwards. During this time, dubbed The Antiquity of Crimson's reign, vast sums of potential were dragged to Luthwardron.

What was once a war-torn land, was fertilized by the blood of its combatants.

Crimson's hunger was his own undoing- an ironic fate- as his lust for power brought about the righteous climate for The Birth.

Once, just once, every eon- the stars align. The Divinity orders itself as it once was; perfect. In that perfection, though lasting no more than an instant, potential was seeded into the ground of Luthwardron. The fertilized ground, watered by the tears of the damned- gave the perfect climate from which The Vessel of God was born.

Its name was enough to drive back the forces of evil- and its gift was pure. The Vessel of God bestowed upon the many inhabitants of Luthwardron a power so crucial- so terrifying to the Dark Lord- The Vessel bestowed hope. Magicks blossomed like wildflowers- rapidly covering Luthwardron. With the power given to them by The Vessel, the poor slaves of Crimson began one last war.

Man, Elf, Dwarf, Imp, and all other life revolted against the Darkness- and in the end, brought death to its master. [place holder name for the big bad evil guy] was defeated.

As it would have, power finds itself at the heart of all motion in The Divinity. After the fall of [evil guy], the three races which had become most adept with the powerful magicks each formed their own Kingdoms- and together, thrust Luthwardron into its Golden Age. Two Kingdoms of Man- The Greater Luethenia, and The Lesser Cyrianopolis. The Kingdom of Dwarves- Horkraum. And The Kingdom of the Elves- Fynnstaefum.

For many gilded ages, the four kingdoms prospered. Wealth and Power accumulated, Knowledge flourished, and the Magicks that had become so crucial to their freedom were honed.

The Golden Age gave birth to many of the Realm's greatest legends. Heroes were born, and earned their name hunting down the remnant forces of [big bad evil guy].

Pride came before the fall.

The two Kingdoms of Man- once bound together, akin to brothers- separated. They bickered for dwindling land grants- and the once rich soils of Luthwardron became dry. The Dwarves dug deeper than they ever had- allured by the riches hidden within the earth. The Elves secluded themselves, distancing themselves from whom they deemed, lesser races.

War broke out among the Human Kingdoms. Insults turned to skirmishes- Skirmishes to battles- Battles to bloodshed- and Bloodshed- to desperation.

The King of Cyrianopolis, The Honored Viktos, watched as his Kingdom was laid to waste. His court was murdered. His family taken. Relying on his most trusted advisors, Viktos led one final prayer in the innermost chambers of his Castle.

An ancient ritual, a sacrifice, to call upon the Divinity- to bring Salvation and Peace- a legend so old, its true purpose had become buried.

The King, driven to desperation, plunged a sullen, black blade into his heart.

His most trusted advisor, Okhaust of Cyrian, had won. The Ritual would save only one Kingdom, and it would not be Viktos'.

Cyrianopolis sank into the depths- the power forged in the Ritual too strong for the weakened earth to bare- and the earth swallowed it whole- closing the ground beneath it, for ages to come.

At the same moment as the fall of Cyrianopolis- the Dwarven King, Hakread, driven to near-insanity by his all-too-dwarven greed, split the earth, leagues below the surface. A blackness, which swallowed even the light from their torches, stared back at the Dwarven King. How cunning was the Dark lord, to leave this, for the dwarves to find. The Dwarven King, realizing the Rapture he had brought about, led one final act of Dwarven Courage. With all the might of his forefathers, King Hakread plummeted his Kingdom into the earth- to serve as the only seal between unimaginable horrors, and the surface-world he hadn't seen in years.

Horkraum was buried, along with many of its peoples and heroes.

The secluded elven city was the last to be touched by this fateful moment. For, at the same moment as the Human and Dwarven Kingdoms fell, an order was signed into motion by the Elven Liege.

Ages ago, there were more than the fair-skinned elves we know today. There were another culture- whom lived intertwined in the Elven kingdom. The White Elves and 'Sullen Elves', as they were called in the later days of the Golden Age.

The Elven Liege, in a time of great tension, ordered the execution of all Sullen Elves.

More blood soaked the land.

Luethenia was the last of the Old Kingdoms to fall.

Lured by the prospect of reclaiming their lost birthrights- Dwarven and Human forces plunged their city of Orvandy into the earth. The misguided citystate dug for many years- and, ironically, by the hand of the old Dawrven King's lineage, the lost Kingdom of Cryianopolis was uncovered. Its riches had spoiled, and its peoples had decayed- but even deeper within the chasm- the festering hatred of Okhaust was fed new life. His power of unmatched- he was quick to destroy the forces that had freed his imprisonment within the earth. Orvandy fell before the sun set- many fleeing- and those who had not, being killed.

Okhaust was quick to act- besieging the dying Kingdom of Luethenia mere weeks after his revival. Their crops dead, Magicks dwindling, and provinces lost- Luethenia fell to Okhaust.

The self-proclaimed Demon King burned a path of destruction straight into the last Kingdom of man. Okhaust's march saw the end of Luethenia. The Demon King laid waste to all- rapturing the great mountains of Luthwardron- covering the Realm is a sullen smog and ash.

For ages forlorn, Okhaust ruled. The actions of his reign, to this day, leave wanderlost souls and spirits- cursed to roam the land of Luthwardron. Okhaust's reign, the Ashen Fall (name subject to change), returned the land to a low-point it had seen only once before. The bloodsoaked lands cried out for salvation- nightly prayers cast into the heavens above.

And from the heavens above, came their savior.

Some say he descended from the stars on a chariot of light.

Other accounts claim he sailed a great vessel across an aurora.

It matters not by what mode he arrived on- but merely the fact he did.

Alstalta, the second Vessel of God, brought with him a gift the peoples of Luthwardron so desperately needed.

The same gift the first Vessel had brought.

Hope.

From this hope, a new age of Magicks dawned on Luthwardron. Once again, like eons past, the Vessel of God led a ferocious battle against evil- and as fate would have it, defeated Okhaust. In the end, neither survived. Alstalta ultimately sacrificed himself to seal the Mountains Okhaust had split open- freeing the land of its ashen curse.

Of course, that's just what the believers say.

No man truly knows.

Luthwardron is an empire of Man- thriving upon the prosperity brought by peacetimes- “Pax Luthwardria”. The Magicks flourish, as do the cultures of our Land. Tales of demonic armies and ashen winters are frowned upon. Naught more than children's stories- or, perhaps, were. In recent days, any mention of The Vessel of God, or The Demon King, Okhaust, are silenced by those in power. Protecting the Empire, from false truths, they claim.

Many of us Scholars, hidden in our studies, far from the Empire's grasp, still speak the untold knowledge of our forefathers.

The Ceaseless Wheel spins, despite man's best efforts to stop it.
